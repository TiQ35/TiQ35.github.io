Fe-alginate microgels via droplet microfluidics

Controlling microgel morphology and Fe³⁺ release with droplet composition — experimental work that led to two publications

Status: completed · Beijing Jiaotong University, Prof. Yuandu Hu’s group · 2024 – 2025

The problem

Alginate microgels crosslinked with Fe³⁺ are attractive for drug delivery and soft materials because their structure — and therefore their release behavior — can be tuned. But how droplet composition during microfluidic fabrication maps onto final microgel morphology was not well characterized.

What I did

Using droplet microfluidics, we fabricated Fe-alginate microgels and systematically studied how fabrication conditions control the outcome:

  • Investigated the effects of droplet composition, viscosity, and FeCl₃ concentration on microgel morphology and deformation.
  • Characterized the resulting structures with SEM and optical microscopy.
  • Contributed to a follow-up study on spherical magnetic Fe-alginate microgels made with an external crosslinking approach, including their pH-dependent Fe³⁺ release behavior.

Outcome

This work resulted in two co-authored papers: one in Lab on a Chip (2025) on morphology control, and one in the Chinese Journal of Polymer Science (2025) on magnetic spherical microgels and controlled release — see publications.

It was also my entry point into research: it taught me how real materials behave outside of idealized models, an intuition I now carry into computational work on ion transport.
